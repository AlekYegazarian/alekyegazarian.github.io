About

Alek is a Visiting Lecturer of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Loyola Marymount University and a Principal Digital Engineer at Northrop Grumman.

His research interests lie at the intersection of computational imaging, medical devices, and optics. Within the community, Alek serves as Vice Chair of the LMU ECE Board of Advisors and is also a magician member of The Academy of Magical Arts at the Magic Castle in Hollywood. Previously, Alek was an engineer in the Flight Software and Avionics Systems group at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory, where he worked on the Europa Clipper spacecraft.

Alek is a recipient of the Ming Hsieh Outstanding Academic Achievement Award and served as the 2025 Master's Student Commencement Speaker for the USC Viterbi School of Engineering.

He received his Master of Science in Electrical Engineering, specializing in Signal and Image Processing, from The University of Southern California, where he worked with Dr. C.-C. Jay Kuo on Green Learning for image denoising and super-resolution. Alek completed his Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering at Loyola Marymount University, graduating Summa Cum Laude with a concentration in Computer Engineering and a Minor in Computer Science, while researching with Dr. Hossein Asghari on real-time volumetric optical coherence tomography.
